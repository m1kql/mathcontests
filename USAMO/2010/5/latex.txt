Let $q = \dfrac{3p-5}{2}$ where $p$ is an odd prime, and let

 \begin{center} \[S_q =    \frac{1}{2\cdot 3 \cdot 4} +   \frac{1}{5\cdot 6 \cdot 7} + \cdots +   \frac{1}{q\cdot (q+1) \cdot (q+2)}.\] \end{center} 

Prove that if $\dfrac{1}{p}-2S_q = \dfrac{m}{n}$ for integers
$m$ and $n$, then $m-n$ is divisible by $p$.
